Gold is a noble metal, meaning it is resistant to most chemical reactions, including reactions with hydrochloric acid (HCl). When gold is exposed to hydrochloric acid alone, there is no observable chemical reaction. However, if you mix hydrochloric acid with another acid, like nitric acid, you can create a mixture called aqua regia. Aqua regia is capable of dissolving gold, forming chloroauric acid (HAuCl4) as a product.

Sinking sand, also known as quicksand, is a type of wet sand that becomes liquefied and viscous when it is disturbed. It can cause a person or object to become trapped. Sinking sand occurs when water-saturated sand or other soil particles are agitated and cannot support weight. The object or person then becomes trapped, but the density of the sand-water suspension exceeds that of the human body, so the body cannot sink below the surface. It is a natural phenomenon found in various regions worldwide. Quicksand is particularly found in areas with high groundwater levels and fine sand, silt, or clay.
