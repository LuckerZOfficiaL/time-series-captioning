The waves produced by the merging of two supermassive black holes can be detected using highly sensitive instruments called gravitational wave detectors.

The merging of two supermassive black holes does not necessarily produce a quasar. Epigenetic inheritance refers to the transmission of traits from one generation to the next that are not encoded in the DNA sequence itself, but rather in the way that the DNA is packaged and regulated. Epigenetic changes can be caused by various environmental factors, such as diet, stress, or exposure to toxins. These changes can then be passed down to future generations through mechanisms such as DNA methylation, histone modification, and non-coding RNA molecules.

There is no scientific evidence to support the concept of "backward epigenetic inheritance," which suggests that the experiences of an individual's descendants can affect the epigenome of that individual's ancestors. Epigenetic inheritance is a phenomenon where environmental factors affect an individual's epigenome, and these changes can be passed down to their offspring, but not in the reverse direction.
